## A conventional Hot War

- Countries directly participate in a war
- Violent state of hostility
- Warfare between many countries
- War in the air and at sea
- Countries hope to have as many colonies as possible
- FIGHT WARS
- Warfare between 2 superpowers


## Examples of a cold war
-  Competition to ensure their political belief dominate
	Leading to:
	- Countries hope to influence as much of the worls as possiblr\e
- Alliances formed to pursue their own interest
	Which may lead to warfare between many countries
- Nuclear arms race as a way to avoid war
	As a form of<mark class="hltr-cyan">  *deterence* </mark>
	If both superpowers have nuclear weapons, there is **Mutually Assured Destruction**, both of them wipe out each other
- Fighting wars with each other using other countries
	Proxy wars
- World divided into two opposing camps with competing social and econ beliefs
- Spying / espionage on each other
- Non-violent state of hositility
- Gathering of resistance groups
- Alliances form in the interest of 2 superpowers
- Use propoganda and mass media to attack each other
- Both sides provided financial and military aid to countries to win theri support
- Countires hope to have as many colonies as possible
- Space war
- Military arms race to have an advantage in a war
