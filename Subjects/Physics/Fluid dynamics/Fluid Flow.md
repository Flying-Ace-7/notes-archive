## Volume flow rate
Flow rate can be described with a quantity, SI Units of $m^3 s^{-1}$
he volume of fluid passing by a given location through an area during a period of time is called **flow rate** $Q$, or more precisely, volume flow rate.
We can hence describe flow rate as:
$$
Q= \frac{dV}{dt}
$$Where V is the volume  and t is elapsed time

We can further describe $V=Ax$, where $A$ is the cross sectional area of the pipe/container, to give the volume of the container.
Hence:
$$
Q=\frac{dV}{dt}=\frac{d}{dt}Ax=A\frac{dx}{dt}=Av
$$
where $v$ is the (average) velocity of the fluid
