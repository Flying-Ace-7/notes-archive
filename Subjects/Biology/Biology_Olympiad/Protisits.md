Protists is the informal name of the kingdom that are **mostly** unicellular eukaryotes that are neither:
- Plants
- Animals, nor
- Fungi

> [!note] Recap: Eukrayotes vs Prokrayotes
> 
|                                       |                                                |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Prokaryotic cells                     | Eukaryotic Cells                               |
| Small and simple                      | Large and complex                              |
| 0.1 to 5.0 micrometeres in size       | 10 to 100 micrometers in size                  |
| Unicellular, no nucleus               | Unicellular or Multicellular, Nulcleus Present |
| Circular DNA, Haploid chromosomes (n) | Linear DNA, Diploid chromosomes (2n)           |
| **Lack membrane-bound organelles**    | **Contain membrane-bound organelles**          |
| Cell division by binary fission       | Cell division by mitosis                       |

Protists are hard to classify.
_Domain Protista_ is polyphyletic, where members are descended from different ancestors.
They can either be autotrophic, Heterotrophic, or mixotrophic, plant-like, animal-like or fungus like respectively.
They can reproduce asexually or sexually.
They are a wide range of organisms.

Some protists reproduce sexually, through [Meiosis]([[Heredity I#Meiosis]]) or fertilisation. Some examples include the conjugation of paramecium, which is a sexual process.

## Protist evolution
### Endosymbiosis
Much of protist diversity has its origins in endosymbiosis.
Endosymbiosis -> process in which certain unicellular organisms engulf other cells, which become endosymbionts and ultimately organelles in host cells. Some examples inculde chloroplasts and mitochondrion in plant and animal cells respectively.

Mitochondira orginated from an aerobic prokaryote becoming an endosymbiont in a larger prokaryote.
Chloroplats evolved from cyanobacterium, becoming and endosymbiont in a larger prokrayote

#### Example: Hatena artenicola
It is a micro-organism that has a complex feeding apparatus to obtain food. Through endosymbiosis, it engulfs algae, which photosynthesises and provides food for itself and the hatena arenciola. _Hatnea_ stops feeding, and the feeding apparatus changes into a light-sensing organelle, guiding them to light for faster rate of photosynthesis. 
During reproduction through binary fission, the _Hatena_ splits, and the algae remains in only one of the daughter cells. The other cells remains as a predator, until it engulfs another algae.


### Secondary endosymbiosis

A cynobacterium was engulfed by a heterotrophic eukaryote (primary endosymbiosis) to produce and ancestor with chloroplasts which diversified into:
- red algae
- green algae
Red and green algae were subsequently engulfed by other eukaryotes (Secondary endosymbiosis), which resulted in other types of protists, who where Mixotrophs.
