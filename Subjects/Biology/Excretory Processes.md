The function of the excretory system is to _remove nitrogenous waste and other toxic waste products from our body._
Mainly, the waste gathered through [[Metabolism]], and metabolic processes.



### Excretory processes:


- Excretion
	Removal of metabolic waste products, toxic substances, and substances  in excess of the body's requirement
- Egestion
	- Removal of unwanted/undigested material 
- Secretion
	- release of substances useful for the body (eg, enzymes)
	- Some examples of secretion include Eating (saliva), sweating (water to cool body down)


#### Organs involved in excretion include:

- Lungs
	- CO_2 and H2O in expired air
- Liver
	- Bile pigments in faceas through the intestine
- Skin (Sweat glands)
	- Excess Urea, salts and water
- Kidneys (Focus of this topic)
	- Excess Urea, salts and water too


> [!tip] Importance of excretion
> Harmful wastes produced during some metabollic processes in the body needs to be excreted out of the body, along with toxic substances, and excess substances.
> This is to prevent the buildup/accumulation of such substances in the body, to a level which is harmful to the body.

Protein and nucleic acid metabolism produces Nitrogenous wastes, which has different forms:
- Ammonia 
- Urea
- Uric Acid

They all have different amounts of toxicity, relative amount of water required for excretion, and relative amount of energy required fro production.
We can summarise it in the table below.


|                   |                      |                                                 |                                                       |
| ----------------- | -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Nitrogenous waste | Relative toxicity    | relative amount of water required for excretion | relative amount of energy/ATP required fro production |
| Ammonia (NH3)     | High                 | High                                            | Low                                                   |
| Urea              | Low                  | Moderate                                        | Moderate                                              |
| Uric acid         | Very low (Non-toxic) | Low                                             | High                                                  |

Due to these factors, different organisms will evolve to produce different nitrogenous wastes, depending on which is the most suitable for them.
For eg:

Fish will produce and excrete Ammonia. They are in water, and hence, have basically unlimited water to use for excretion, hence, Ammonia is suitable foe them, as it also requires less energy to be produced. 

(Analogy)Basically, imagin the fish thinking that "_I have so much water around me! why would i use more ATP to produce urea, when i can jsut be done with ammonia. idrc about the amount of water needed, cuz i have a lot._"

Mammals will need some ATP to produce Urea. (Urea production in Liver -> Urea excreted in Kidney)
While, reptiles in dry environments will use the most ATP and produce Uric acid, as their water is limited.


>[!question] Why can't all organisms excrete ammonia
>Urea is produced in the liver. If ammonia was produced instead, it will take too long for it to be excreted, making it too toxic to the body. A lot of water will eb required to dilute it.
