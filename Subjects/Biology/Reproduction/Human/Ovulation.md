Ovulation is the relase of a mature egg cell from the ovary. 

## What is ovulation triggered by?

- A High amount of Lutenising Hormone


## Oogenesis
 This process starts with a diploid primary follicle. A high amount of FSH Triggers the development of the follice into a Graafian haploid follicle. Graafian follicle breaks down at Day 14 to release an egg cell into the fallopian tube from the ovary, and turns into a Corpus Leutuem, which produces [[Oestrogen]] and [[Progesterone]]





###  **Days 1–5: Menstrual Phase**

- **Ovary:**  
    The **ovarian cycle** is in its early **follicular phase**. FSH (follicle-stimulating hormone) from the pituitary starts stimulating several follicles to grow. Oestrogen and progesterone are both **low**, because the corpus luteum from the previous cycle has broken down.
- **Uterus:**  
    The **uterine cycle** is in the **menstrual phase**. The drop in hormones causes the **endometrium (uterine lining)** to shed, leading to menstrual bleeding.

---

###  **Days 6–13: Follicular / Proliferative Phase**

- **Ovary:**  
    The follicles continue to mature, and one becomes **dominant**. This follicle secretes **increasing amounts of estrogen**, which helps regulate FSH and prepares the body for ovulation.
- **Uterus:**  
    Under the influence of rising oestrogen, the **proliferative phase** begins. The **endometrium rebuilds**, blood vessels grow, and the lining thickens in preparation for a possible pregnancy.

---

###  **Day 14: Ovulation**

- **Ovary:**  
    When oestrogen levels remain high for about two days, they trigger a sudden **surge of LH ([[Lutenising Hormone]])** (Oestrogen provide +ve feedback to LH)from the pituitary gland. This **LH surge causes ovulation** — the **release of a mature egg** from the ovary. The empty follicle becomes the **corpus luteum**.
- **Uterus:**  
    Around this time, the endometrium is fully developed and ready to support an embryo. Cervical mucus becomes thinner, helping sperm travel more easily for fertilization.

---

###  **Days 15–28: Luteal / Secretory Phase**

- **Ovary:**  
    The **corpus luteum** secretes **progesterone** (and some estrogen). If no fertilization occurs, the corpus luteum begins to degenerate after about 10–12 days.
- **Uterus:**  
    This is the **secretory phase**. Progesterone makes the **endometrium thicker, softer, and nutrient-rich**, preparing for implantation of a fertilized egg. If the egg is not fertilized, progesterone levels drop as the corpus luteum breaks down, causing the uterine lining to shed — returning to the **menstrual phase**.
